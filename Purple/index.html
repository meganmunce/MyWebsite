about me

After growing up in the San Francisco Bay Area, I went to college at Northwestern's Medill School of Journalism, Media, Integrated Marketing Communications and earned both my B.S. and M.S. in journalism through Medill's Accelerated Masters Program.

During college, I took classes in computer science, Spanish, political science and legal studies, while also working at The Daily Northwestern and serving as president of Northwestern's nationally renound mock trial team.

I also interned doing audience engagement research and data analysis for KQED, the Bay Area's NPR and PBS member station, and did two fellowships with The Texas Tribune: one in reporting, and another in audience engagement.

Now, I'm a 2022-2024 Hearst Fellow completing my first rotation as a business and breaking news reporter for The Houston Chronicle.
